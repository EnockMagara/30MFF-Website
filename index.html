THE POEM

INSPIRED BY CAMPUS IN 90 SECONDS

What if the campus could speak?

Not with words, but with breath—

With rustling leaves and morning wheels,

With clanks and chirps and laughter that lingers

In the corners where memories nest.

The elevator sighs, a metal yawn,

Lifting stories floor by floor—

A pause, a groan, a journey up—

It speaks in gears, in stops and starts,

In the language of in-between.

Outside, the sky holds an orchestra:

Birdsong weaves through engine hums,

Treetops whisper ancient tunes,

And even silence holds a tone

If you dare to lean in.

Footsteps echo in open halls,

Each one a footnote in the day.

Doors beep—a gentle chorus—

And open into spaces

Where voices bloom like morning light.

At Baraha, the rhythm shifts—

A sudden spark, a clash of joy,

Where clinks of ping pong meet laughter's rise,

And the spirit of friendly battle

Sings in playful defiance.

Then comes D2, the midday heart—

Where trays collide like cymbals,

And stories stretch across the tables

In bites, in beats, in bites again—

A melody of mouths and music.

The marketplace hums a different song,

A weaving of casual tunes and passing chatter,

Where you move not just with feet

But with ears wide open

To the subtle percussion of presence.

This campus does not sit still—

It lives, it moves, it speaks in waves.

Its language is not heard by all,

But it is felt by those who listen.

So listen—

To the buzz behind the quiet,

To the heartbeat in the hum.

Let the echoes wrap around your ribs,

Let the soundtrack of place

Become a part of your own rhythm.

Every wall remembers,

Every bench breathes,

Every breeze carries a conversation

That needs no translation.

And in these 90 seconds,

Time folds itself into sound,

Memory walks beside movement,

And meaning hums low under it all.

Even in silence, the campus sings.

ABOUT

Campus in 90 Seconds: A Sound Story is a personal reflection on what it feels like to move through NYUAD, not just what we see but what we hear and remember.

Without a single word spoken, the familiar sounds begin to speak for themselves. Clinking elevator doors, birdsong drifting through the air, the quiet buzz of Baraha, and the warmth of voices and music in D2 and the Marketplace each become part of a unique language. These sounds express the rhythm and life of the campus in their own way.

These sounds are not just background noise; they are markers of moments, friendships, and routines that shape our days. This film captures the quiet hum of campus life and turns it into a memory in motion.

It is a way of listening deeply to the campus I call home and inviting others to do the same. Even in silence, the campus speaks. You just have to listen.
